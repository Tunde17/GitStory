Gulyás Soup (Goulash)

I visited many countries and I noticed (especially in the western and northern parts of Europe) that when I ordered ‘gulyás’ (“Goulash”) in a restaurant, I got a stew. Originally, ‘gulyás’ is a traditional Hungarian soup, you can find it in every single traditional restaurant in Hungary or in big festivals. The name ‘gulyás’ means “herdsman”, however, he watches over only a herd of cows (not sheep, etc.) as in Hungarian there is a different word for a herdsman who watches over e.g. a herd of sheep or horses. The meaning is very important in this case, because the main ingredient of this soup is beef –surprise, surprise - because of the herdsman. The herdsman basically lived his life on the fields, the original ‘gulyás’ is made outside on open fire in a big cauldron called ‘bogrács’.

What I used:

  • 1 kg beef
  • 3 red onions
  • 2 carrots
  • 1 celery
  • 1 tbs sweet paprika
  • 0.5 tbs hot paprika
  • 0.5 tbs cumin
  • 0.5 kg potatoes
  • 1 small tomato
  • 1 tbs salt
  • 0.5 tsp black pepper
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 3 gloves of garlic
  • 2 tsp lard
  • a bunch of parsley
  • 2.5 l water

How to make it:

Cut the onions into small cubes and the beef into large cubes. Heat the lard in a large pot and cook the onions until soft, stirring frequently. Add the sweet and hot paprika powder to the onion, stir it for a few minutes, then add the sliced beef and cook until it has a whitish coat.

Pour in approximately 0.2 l of water, garlic, salt, pepper, cumin, bay leaves, tomato and cook it for 80-90 minutes.

Peel and cut the carrots, celery and potatoes into large cubes and add it to the beef. Pour in 2.5 l water and cook it for another hour.

Serve it with parsley on top.
